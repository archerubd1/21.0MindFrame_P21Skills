The Minimum Payment Trap

The most expensive way to use credit: Servicing interest vs. reducing debt.

Trap Formula: Low Payment = High Interest + (Tiny Principal)

How the Trap Works

Interest First: Banks apply your payment to accrued interest first. Only the leftovers touch the balance.

Treading Water: Principal reduces by pennies, ensuring next month's interest charge remains almost exactly the same.

Perpetual Debt: Paying only minimums extends a small purchase into a decade-long obligation.

Real World Cost

Scenario: $1,200 Laptop @ 22% APR

Min Payment: $24 (2%)

TIME TO REPAY 10+ Yrs
TOTAL INTEREST $1,000+

You pay nearly double the price.

Escape Strategy

Ideal: Pay Statement Balance in full every month. Interest = $0.

The 3x Rule: If you must carry a balance, pay at least 3x the minimum due.

Fixed Payments: Don't pay a %. Set a fixed sum (e.g., $100/mo) until it's gone.

Stop Spending: If carrying a balance, new purchases often accrue interest immediately (no grace period).
