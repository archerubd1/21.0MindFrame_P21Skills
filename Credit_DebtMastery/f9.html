Compound Interest Impact

The double-edged sword: it either builds your wealth or deepens your debt.

A = P(1 + r/n)nt (P=Principal, r=Rate, n=Frequency, t=Time)

Debt Cost Example

Scenario: $2,000 Credit Card Debt

  • APR: 24% (High Interest)
  • Compounds: Monthly
  • Action: No payments for 12 mos

New Balance After 1 Year

$2,537 (+$537 Interest)

Savings Growth Example

Scenario: Invest $50/month

  • APY: 5% (Conservative)
  • Frequency: Monthly
  • Duration: 3 Years

Future Value

~$1,890 (Growth)

Decision Rule

"Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it... he who doesn't... pays it."

Action:

Never hold high-interest debt longer than absolutely necessary. Let time work FOR you.
