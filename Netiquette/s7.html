Global Communication

  • Cultural Awareness: Be culturally sensitive and avoid local idioms, jargon, or slang that may not translate well.
  • Time Zones: Always mind global time differences and respect varying regional expectations for response times.
  • Language Clarity: Use clear, simple English and proactively confirm mutual understanding to prevent miscommunication.
  • Respect & Tone: Respect professional titles and pronouns, and avoid humor or sarcasm that might be misinterpreted globally.
