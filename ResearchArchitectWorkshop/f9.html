1

Define keywords + synonyms

List 2–4 main terms, then add synonyms and related phrases.

teen OR adolescent

2

Choose the right database

Pick a database that matches your subject (e.g., DOAJ, ERIC).

DOAJ • ERIC • Library DB

3

Build the query with operators

Combine synonyms with OR, connect ideas with AND, exclude with NOT.

(adolescent OR teen) AND sleep

4

Apply filters strategically

Use date range, type, subject, and peer-review filters to reduce noise.

Year • Type • Peer-review

5

Scan titles + abstracts first

Quickly judge relevance before opening full PDFs or saving items.

Title → Abstract → Keywords

6

Save, cite, and export results

Bookmark items, download citations, and record DOI/URL for your bibliography.

RIS • BibTeX • Copy citation

Pro move: after 10 results, refine one thing (term, filter, or database) and run the search again.

Quality checkpoints

Prefer peer-reviewed or editorially reviewed sources.

Use quotes for exact phrases (names, programs, key concepts).

Track what you changed each search (term, filter, or database).

Mini toolkit (what to look for)

Filters: year, subject, type, peer-review

Save: folders, alerts, reading lists

Cite: DOI, export formats, citation tools
