Encryption Basics — Purpose & Key Concepts

Why Encrypt?

To ensure confidentiality, integrity, authenticity, and safety of sensitive data both at rest and in transit across networks.

The Core Principle

Mathematical guarantees protect your information even when physical devices are stolen or networks are compromised.

Key Concepts

Symmetric vs. Asymmetric: Single shared key for both locking/unlocking vs. distinct public and private key pairs.

End-to-End Encryption (E2EE): Only the communicating end users hold the keys; intermediaries cannot read the data.

Key Management: Encompasses strong passphrases, secure key storage, routine rotation, and recovery keys.

Hashing vs. Encryption: Hashing verifies integrity (one-way), while encryption provides confidentiality (two-way).

Transport vs. Storage: Transport encryption (TLS) protects moving data; storage encryption (FDE/files) protects saved data.
