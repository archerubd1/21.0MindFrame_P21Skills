Case Study: India Launch

Applying the Strategic Evidence framework to a real-world scenario

Context

A mid-sized SaaS company is planning to launch a new project management tool in the Indian market. They need to validate the market potential and competitive landscape before committing budget.

Tools Applied

Primary data gathering platforms utilized for this analysis:

Semrush
Statista
  • Semrush: Analyzed search volume for "project management software" in India and identified top 5 organic competitors.
  • Statista: Retrieved data on internet penetration growth, mobile usage stats, and SME digitalization trends in India.

Evaluation (CRAAP)

  • Currency: Filtered all data for post-2023 to ensure relevance to current digital infrastructure.
  • Accuracy: Cross-referenced Statista growth projections with World Bank economic reports to verify consistency.
  • Bias Check: Discarded a "Market Boom" report sponsored by a competitor due to potential conflict of interest.

Strategic Recommendation

Go/No-Go Decision: GO - Phased Approach

Based on the evidence that Tier-1 cities drive 70% of demand but show high price sensitivity:

  • Localization: Essential. Offer UI in English but support content in Hindi/Hinglish.
  • Pricing: Implement a "freemium" model to capture the SME sector identified in Statista data.
  • Monitoring: Track brand search volume monthly via Semrush to gauge traction.
