Red Flags

Critical indicators of unreliable or manipulative evidence

No Sources Identified

Claims made without verifiable citations, footnotes, or links to original data. If you cannot trace the data back to its origin, it cannot be trusted for strategic decisions.

Clear Bias

Content that uses strong emotional language, one-sided arguments, or ignores counter-evidence. Always check for potential conflicts of interest in the author's background.

Buzzword Overload

Excessive use of complex jargon (e.g., "synergy," "paradigm shift," "disruptive") without concrete definitions. Often used to mask a lack of substantive evidence or methodology.

Sponsored Research

Studies funded by entities with a vested interest in a specific outcome. While not always false, these require extra scrutiny regarding data selection and omitted variables.
