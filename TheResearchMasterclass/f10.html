Plagiarism

Definition

Using another author’s work without proper credit—whether you copy it directly or rewrite it without citing the source.

Why it matters

Protects fairness and respects original authorship.

Maintains trust in academic work and evidence.

Builds credibility: readers can verify your sources.

Quick self-check

If you copied exact wording, use quotation marks and cite.

If the idea came from someone else, cite—even when paraphrasing.

If it’s your prior work, ask permission or disclose reuse (as required).
