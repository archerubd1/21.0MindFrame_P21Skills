Citation Styles

APA

Common in psychology, education, and social sciences. Emphasizes author and year for research currency.

MLA

Common in literature and the humanities. Emphasizes author and page number for close reading.

Chicago

Common in history and some social sciences. Uses Notes & Bibliography or Author–Date systems.

Example format (APA — journal article)

Use as a model

Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article. Journal Title, volume(issue), pages. https://doi.org/xx.xxxxxx

1

Match the order exactly: Author → Year → Title → Source → DOI/URL.

2

Use italics for the journal title and volume (style rules vary by guide).

3

Keep punctuation consistent—small formatting mistakes can break credibility.
