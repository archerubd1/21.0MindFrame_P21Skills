Research Ethics

No copying

Use your own words and structure. If you quote or closely follow a source, clearly mark it and cite it.

Give credit

Cite ideas, data, images, and exact wording—even when you paraphrase—so authors receive proper acknowledgement.

Use trusted sources

Prefer peer-reviewed articles, academic books, and reputable institutions to strengthen accuracy and credibility.

Ethics checklist

Can someone verify where each key claim came from?

Do quotations have quotation marks and citations?

Is the source credible and appropriate for academic use?

Tip

When in doubt, cite: it’s better to over-credit than to risk accidental plagiarism.
